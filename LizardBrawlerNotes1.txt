🥊 What the Brawler Brings (from Advanced Class Guide)
Even though you said “mostly Core,” this class is so close to your theme that it’s worth analyzing in case your GM permits it.

Core Strengths of Brawler
Full BAB — same as Barbarian.

Improved Unarmed Strike baked in (and scaling damage like a Monk).

Brawler’s Flurry — allows two-weapon fighting with unarmed strikes (using full BAB progression).

Martial Flexibility — the gem: temporarily gain any combat feat for a few rounds, a few times per day.

Bonus Feats — from the combat list at 2nd, 5th, 8th, etc.

Close Weapon Group — treats unarmed strikes and close weapons as one group for feat synergies.

💢 What You’d Lose from Barbarian
When you multiclass out of Barbarian, you’re giving up:

Rage Power progression (delays Beast Totem, Pounce, etc.)

Rage rounds per day

Damage Reduction

The scaling Strength/Con bonuses from rage

Movement bonus progression

This is a big trade. The farther you go from Barbarian 10+, the more you lose the animalistic scaling and the Pounce + Natural Attack Monster Combo that makes you terrifying.

Unarmed Strike damage scaling → 1d6 → 1d8 by mid levels.

Flurry lets you full-attack with more strikes at full BAB.

Martial Flexibility lets you dynamically adapt feats every fight — incredible utility.

Still Rage for +STR/+CON during combat (those bonuses apply to all attacks).

Still keep Beast Totem claws for flavor and versatility.

You can even Rage + Flurry together — completely legal, since Flurry is just an alternate full-attack routine.

💀 Savage Barbarian (APG)
Theme:
A truly feral warrior who fights without armor — a primal hunter rather than a disciplined soldier.
This is the archetype for a wild, bare-chested, rage-driven fighter who relies on sheer toughness and fury to shrug off hits.

🧩 Class Feature Changes
Replaces	With
Armor Proficiency	Loses proficiency with medium and heavy armor (light armor only).
Fast Movement	Gains Fast Movement at level 1 that still works while wearing armor.
Damage Reduction (DR)	Loses DR/– entirely.
Uncanny Dodge / Improved Uncanny Dodge	Unchanged.
Trap Sense	Becomes Savage Resilience — gain DR/– equal to half your Trap Sense bonus (so +1 at 3rd, +2 at 6th, etc.) when not wearing armor or using shields.
⚙️ What It Means Practically
Pros:

Keeps your Fast Movement and improves it slightly for light armor use.

You can wear light armor (e.g. hide or chain shirt) and still move freely.

Savage Resilience gives a small DR/– bonus if you fight unarmored.

Fits the flavor of a bare-chested, scarred Lizardfolk warrior who doesn’t need steel to survive.

Keeps full Rage Power access — works fine with Beast Totem.

Perfect synergy with your unarmed “brawler from the depths” theme.

Cons:

Loses full-scale Barbarian DR. Invulnerable Rager will tank better.

You’ll have slightly lower AC if you go fully unarmored (and even with light armor, it’s modest).

Not ideal if your campaign leans into attrition or ranged combat (you’ll get hit more often).

Seafaring / Pirate theme	Excellent flavor fit — swimming and climbing are easier with lighter armor; describes a “sea reptile” barbarian aesthetic.
