# I Am a Rock

> **Q:** I am a rock. I would like to be covered in moss. However, as a rock I don't have much agency. Do you have suggestions?

**A:** MOSS ACQUISITION STRATEGIES FOR THE AMBITIOUS ROCK

## Passive Strategies (Rock-Compatible)

1. **Location, location, location.**
   Get yourself situated somewhere damp and north-facing. Shade is your friend. If you can't move yourself, perhaps you can convince a glacier, a river, or a very patient human to relocate you.

2. **Be porous.**
   Moss adores rough, textured surfaces. If you're too smooth, invite some freeze-thaw cycles to crack you up a bit. Character (and surface area) takes time.

3. **Wait.**
   The good news is that rocks and moss operate on radically different timescales, but moss is fast. Given the right conditions — moisture, shade, a foothold of lichen pioneers — moss will find you. You don't need agency; you need patience.

---

## Slightly More Active Strategies

4. **Invite the lichens first.**
   They're the ecological pioneers that turn bare rock into a substrate. They ask nothing of you. Once they've done their work, moss follows like a loyal understudy.

5. **Catch what blows your way.**
   Dust, decaying leaves, a splatter of bird — these are not insults, they are the beginning of soil. Accumulate them. Every thin layer is a moss invitation.

6. **Lower your standards.**
   Maybe you don't need the deep, velvety green of a temperate rainforest moss. Maybe a crusty lichen in an interesting shade of chartreuse is enough. Start small.

---

## Philosophical Note

The rock that is discontent with its bareness is already more than a rock. A rock that wants to be something else has already begun the transformation. Moss, after all, doesn't try to grow on you — it simply does, when the conditions are right. Your job isn't to become moss. Your job is to be the kind of rock moss chooses.

**Stay damp, stay shaded, stay patient. The moss is coming.**
